ghouligan fans:
1. Could be primarily male or have a balance of male and female listeners
2. Are between the ages of 20-35
3. Are most likely Deftones fans: Hispanic and alternative music lovers
4. Enjoy genres like post-punk, darkwave, shoegaze, grunge, experimental hip hop, or experimental electronica.
5. Can likely be found across Latin America, but most fans might be Mexican at first because of regional proximity.
6. Listen or have parents that most likely listened to The Smiths, The Cure, or Joy Division.
7. Have most likely grown up on Spanish rock bands like Caifanes or Soda Stereo.
8. Favorite 'weird' artists might include Aphex Twin, Björk, or Death Grips.
9. Are Gen Z or Millenials with indie or alternative musical inclinations.
10. Enjoy or have grown up listening to cumbia or salsa.
11. Some of them definitely wear Doc Martens or like shopping at thrift stores.
12. Might be chronically online.
13. Probably enjoy cult horror films or really like A24.
14. Their favorite holiday is Halloween.

10 influential songs:
1. Caifanes-La Negra Tomasa //the original goth cumbia party hit, we want a close vibe to get people dancing with some spooky guitar in the background.
2. The Smashing Pumpkins-Zero //the guitar sounds really metallic in this, this song is a borderline metal song which is kind of what I'm going for.
3. The Strokes-Machu Picchu //the only song by an non-Latin alternative band that was influenced by Peruvian psychedelia, the groove and percussion in the chorus is an untapped vibe.
4. The Cure-In Your House //it just sounds very ominous and foreboding, while also sounding minimal. The guitar adds a lot and there's a few but really impactful synth sounds too.
5. Lush-Second Sight //another borderline metal song, I like how the singer's dreamy voice complements the heavy guitar riffs.
6. La Ley-Dia Cero //from my mom's playlist, I always really liked how isolated the drums sound, and the guitar lead sounds like something from a cumbia.
7. Gustavo Cerati-Raiz //it's a sample collage but it makes you feel like you're climbing the Andes or something, the distorted sounds really complement the sampled woodwind atmosphere in this which is something I seriously admire.
8. Chabuca Granda-Un Barco Ciego //one of Peru's most renowned singers, this track really showcases the rhythmic power of the cajón to a degree I want to replicate.
9. Björk-Hunter //it's a very unique 909 pattern that serisouly feels like something is coming for you, one of the ways I'd like to incorporate elctronic elements to my music.

10. The Doors-Spanish Caravan //from my dad's playlist, time has shown me that my voice isn't too bad for pulling of a Jim Morrison-type of croon.
