Assignment 4

This one’s called error909. I settled on the name because the bassline first made me think about a computer error.
Error 404, the 909 drums, the idea was already there. I ended up recycling the bassline from a previous track I made before.
I really like how this one came out and I wanted to see if I could give it new life by using it under a different genre.
I’m a big fan of adding flanger to things and when I heard how gritty and distorted the bass synth was, all I could think of was adding flanger and lowering the cutoff a bit.

The 909 drums were inspired by an early Justice track,
I just tweaked the drum pattern a bit and ended up getting something just as heavy as my bassline.
I liked where this was going.

Although I had something heavy going on, I knew I needed some chords in there;
I really wanted something soft so I started with a preset called ‘Monday Morning’ on Ableton’s Drift synth.
I liked how ‘faint’ and ‘subtle’ the sound was because it contrasted well with the heavy drums and bass,
so I played around with keys in the A minor scale (I think I might have gotten out of the scale a bit) to get the progression I ended up with.

Even though the track minimum was three, I added a fourth track because I really wanted to experiment with musical contrasts.
They way I approach my own original drum patterns is somewhat shaped by the Afro-Peruvian cajon rhythms I grew up with.
I wanted to add some Latin percussion along the way and I came up with a somewhat-groovy conga pattern. I’m no percussion expert but I am a close listener,
so I came up with the beat by randomly playing some conga sounds and cleaning/organizing the result into something ‘groovy.’
The end result reminded me of some 80s or 90s acid house track I heard somewhere,
but even though the track was taking a noticeable change in direction, I still liked where it was going.

By this point, I really wanted the listener to feel like they were spinning around in a sort of trance,
almost like the blend of 909s with the conga percussion made them feel like they were spinning in a sort of musical carnival.
I really wanted to bring this feeling to the track so I then focused on panning. I panned the congas a bit more towards the left,
but since the 909 ended up feeling like it needed to come off punchy on both sides, I only panned it a bit towards the left for contrast.
I also panned the pads a bit -now to contrast with the 909. Although I feel like mixing is the trickier part of the production process for me,
I overall feel quite satisfied with where the track ended up.
