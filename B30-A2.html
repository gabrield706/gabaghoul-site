Assignment 2

Be Water My Friend.
This is what I decided to name this track, even though it's made entirely of loops that aren't mine
(I guess, if they're royalty-free then maybe I could get away with it, if Justice used loops in some of their songs, why can't I)?
Regardless of this, I thought this was going to be a bit of a challenge because of all loops I would need to listen to.
What I did about this though, was to simply settle on a combination of 2-3 loops I'd find and add others around that.
This felt very similar to putting Lego pieces together, and I do enjoy building stuff with Legos.
However, I had some time limitations and knew I couldn't explore way too much, so I endedn up with this phrase:

I found this sample called 'Underwater Synth Progression Bb Minor 85 BPM', I really liked how atmospheric it was.
The pad sound reminded me of something from a 90s Sega underwater level, or a Frank Ocean song. I wanted to build of of that.
I should mention that I used the keyword 'BPM' to search for loops, just to get a feel for loops without necessarily having to listen to them all.
Eventually, I found a loop called 'Pluck Pad Progression Dm 121 BPM', liked it, and then I pitched the first loop down to match the second.
Suddenly, I was in the lost city of Atlantis.

After chopping the main loops up a bit, I found a very trip hop-sounding drum beat and added that in as the main rhythm.
There's also a loop from an instrument called a 'pandeiro' which gives the song a shaky, almost exotic feel.
I then added some guitar loops, and it was at that point where the track became less of a trip hop track and more of a 'lofi beats to study with' kind of track.
Genrewise, this isn't how I typically approach music-making but I was not at all dissatisfied, I did want to shake things up a bit though, which is why I added the key change.
Now that the song was in full lofi hip hop mode, something was missing. I got to a point where the track felt like it needed indication that the vibe was going to change.
This is where things really got intereting for me, because I started looking for effects loops and got creative with how to complement the vibes with them.
I found a vinyl scratch loop which I felt would fit pretty well, I was a bit sour about it because I felt it made the section changes a little too obvious.
I also used a wind sample and a synth noise sample, to add ocean wave and underwater textures respectively.
Overall, I do feel pretty satisfied with the result, I ended up using 9 samples total and everything sounds very cohesive.
