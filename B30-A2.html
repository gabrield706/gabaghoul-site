Assignment 2

Be Water My Friend.
This is what I decided to name this track, even though it's made entirely of loops that aren't mine
(I guess, if they're royalty-free then maybe I could get away with it, if Justice used loops in some of their songs, why can't I)?
Regardless of this, I thought this was going to be a bit of a challenge because of all loops I would need to listen to.
What I did about this though, was to simply settle on a combination of 2-3 loops I'd find and add others around that.
This felt very similar to putting Lego pieces together, and I do enjoy building stuff with Legos.
However, I had some time limitations and knew I couldn't explore way too much, so I endedn up with this phrase:

I found this sample called 'Underwater Synth Progression Bb Minor 85 BPM', I really liked how atmospheric it was.
The pad sound reminded me of something from a 90s Sega underwater level, or a Frank Ocean song. I wanted to build of of that.
I should mention that I used the keyword 'BPM' to search for loops, just to get a feel for loops without necessarily having to listen to them all.
Eventually, I found a loop called 'Pluck Pad Progression Dm 121 BPM', liked it, and then I pitched the first loop down to match the second.
Suddenly, I was in the lost city of Atlantis.

After chopping the main loops up a bit, I found a very trip hop-sounding drum beat and added that in as the main rhythm.
There's also a loop from an instrument called a 'pandeiro' which gives the song a shaky, almost exotic feel.
I then added some guitar loops, and it was at that point where the track became less of a trip hop track and more of a 'lofi beats to study with' kind of track.
Genrewise, this isn't how I typically approach music-making but I was not at all dissatisfied, I did want to shake things up a bit though, which is why I added the key change.
Now that the song was in full lofi hip hop mode, something was missing. I got to a point where the track felt like it needed indication that the vibe was going to change.
This is where things really got intereting for me, because I started looking for effects loops and got creative with how to complement the vibes with them.
I found a vinyl scratch loop which I felt would fit pretty well, I was a bit sour about it because I felt it made the section changes a little too obvious.
I also used a wind sample and a synth noise sample, to add ocean wave and underwater textures respectively.
In total, I used a total of 9 loops for the entire track.

Since my initial focus had been on getting some good-sounding phrases going, I felt like I was at a good spot with how everything sounded.
I did want to add some effects while also not taking away from the track's feel too much, but at the same time I felt like some small details could be better.
The synth pads and percussion were right where I wanted them and the effects would have benefitted more from panning, so I focused on the guitars.
The plucked loop was already funky, but if I made it watery-funky then that could have maybe complemented the song's 'underwater' feel.
This is why I ended up choosing a chorus effect for it, and while I set it to where the effect is a bit subtle, it adds a necessary 'spice' to the loop.
I also added some reverb to the riff loop (officially called 'Loveline'), which really made it more 'washy' and helped it bleed into the background more.

This has to have been the toughest part for me, because even though I've heard good uses of fader and panning manipulation before, I strongly felt like sound was at a really good spot.
Starting with the pads, I panned the underwater loop more to the left and the plucked pad more to the right.
I really wanted to create a sense of a surrounding atmosphere, and pushing the underwater loop more to the left felt like it would do that, but I also wanted to balance that out.
I liked where the drum kit was at in terms of volume so I lowered down the pandeiro sample a bit-just to accent the kit more.
With the guitars, I wanted to take advantage of the reverb on the Loveline loop for the song's atmosphere, which is why I panned it left with the underwater loop.
The plucked guitar loop needed to contrast the loveline loop at this stage, so I though of having it serve as a bit of a one-ear ticking pattern to make the song more 'quirky'.
This is why I panned that loop more to the right, and I ended up doing the same with the scratch sample. It really adds to the surprise effect of the scratch loop coming in out of nowhere.
By this point I had a larger focuson the atmospheric sounds playing a bit more towards the left ear while the more melodic and one-off sounds were more to the right.
I wanted to break structure a bit, so I ended up pushing my wind sample a bit more to the left. I found that this added to the 'surprise' factor of the song a bit.
