Blues Assignment

"Kiddie Pool Blues":

VERSE:
I wanna surf but I live in Bakersfield
It's gettin' hot so I guess I'll buy a kiddie pool
Oh oh I'm gonna buy a kiddie pool

Yeah

We ain't got a beach but I'll buy myself a kiddie pool
Oh yeah

While ther have been plenty of songs through time that use the 12-bar blues, I wanted to explore how my voice could sound if I provided it with a different instrumental context. I ususally go for the soft crooning approach, but that didn't feel right when it came to using the 12-bar blues approach (it feels more upbeat than the more dark, spooky stuff I tend to go for). I did my best impression of a blues singer over my electric guitar, added some Garageband drums, and came up with something my grandparents would've probably digged back in the day. I do want to point out that because I didn't have an external mic, I couldn't isolate the metronome when recording the vocals while having my headphones on. Nonetheless, I decided to keep the metronome cowbell for texture.

The lyrics were pretty absurd, this somewhat comes from a place of not being able to fit any instrospective meanings to the vibe of the 12-bar blues, and I do like maintaining a balance of not taking myself too seriously, artistically speaking (I heard Tool are good at doing that and they still feel pretty profound to me). It was hot when I recorded the track so I used that for inspiration-the fact that we're in California but far from the beach felt ironic, and the idea of settling for a kiddie pool felt kind of funny. Overall, I ended up with something that feels like a tongue-in-cheek version of a stereotypical Beach Boys song.
