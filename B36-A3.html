The Artist Vision:
Key words:
Innovative, Thoughtful, Mysterious, Enigmatic, Electronic,
Spiritual, Crooning, Cultural, Genre-defying, Atmospheric,
Introspective, Critical, Absurd, Nostalgic, Pacific,
Semi-romantic, Ominous, Experimental, Sentimental, Occult

I think that first and foremost, my project will stand for artistic innovation
through an emphasis on juxtaposing technology with natural, occult, and philosophical themes.
I stand against one-sisde views on just about any social topic, except that I'm not too sure
this is something I want to conveny through my music yet. Although I'm not too sure
what beliefs my art will fully encompass, I am sure about two things:
1. South American music and artists are practically unrepresented at the global level
2. Technology can still allow artists to creating impactful and meaningful art, but the journey is in figuring out how.

Short Bio:
(This is all hypothetical for this assignment)
Through his alias ghouligan, Gabriel Diaz just wants to blow your mind.
Blending Andean and Afro-Latin rhythms with electronic beats and crooning vocals,
the Peruvian-American singer/songwriter has heads detonating with his captivating new grooves.
As mysterious as the Nazca lines and as vast as Machu Picchu, ghouligan's music takes
listeners on a spiritual journey across a kaleidoscopic array of sounds.
Every new track on every new album is a mountain to climb- with ups and downs but rewarding at the end.

within the span of 6-9 months, ghouligan has amassed a considerable cult following
Boasting 500,000 listeners across Spotify, Apple Music, and SoundCloud, ghouligan
was named "Best New Latin Artist" by Pitchfork. He is set perform on NPR's Tiny Desk Concert
series very soon.

ghouligan is set to releasle his first album.
Titled "Selected Ghouly Works 17-25", listeners can expect to find hits from his prior EPs
in addition to 12 new unreleased tracks.
This marks a big milestone for the Gen Z artist, as a culmination of nearly 10 years
since his first documented demos.
