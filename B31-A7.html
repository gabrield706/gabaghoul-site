Wordplay

"my shining path":

(Pre-Chorus)
And I could wonder if we'd meet again
Had everyone cared in your life
Would we open up if both our paths just
Weren't so different at all?

(Chorus)
Cause if this is your song
I've been making along
Then I've wased my time
On a worthless mind
That could never come cold
If things went foretold
Cause all I ever had
Was a shining path

This is a song I've been wanting to make for a while. To say the least, the lyrics are for a deceased relative. This is as personal as I ever hope to get when writing lyrics.
The reason I came to using this theme was partly to take advantage of the momentum, but also because the idea for the chorus sort of came up one night. I was playing back the instrumental one night and naturally ended up humming some stuff along. That's when the 'this is your song, I've been making along' part came up. I was reluctant to use that rhyme out of respet for the subject matter, but no other words felt right to convey that.
In retrospect, I think the way the rest of the rhymes came out didn't really invoke any notions of lightheartedness. If anything, I think I had a framework to move further along with the song. The next rhyme is that of 'I've wasted my time on a worthless mind.' I really liked how those two words -time and mind- don't actually end the same way but the sound of them still fits. The same could be said about the last line ('All I ever had was a shining path'), the end words don't really rhyme but they just fit. Of course, rhyming 'cold' and 'foretold' is much more straightforward. I did want to use a line other than 'foretold,' but I also ran into this being a case of not having any better words to say what I needed to say.
While the melody structure is practically the same across every set of two lines in the chorus, I am particularly fond of how the pre-chorus breaks the rhyming scheme a bit: the first line has a rhyme between again and cared despite the fact that cared isn't the last word in the phrase. I think it's the way that I sang that phrase that makes the words come off as a rhyme. Then I just don't do it during the next two lines, which is good to me because it keeps things interesting.
