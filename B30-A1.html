Assignment 1

I really enjoyed how Ableton put this series of lessons together.
It reminded me a lot of when I first started learning music theory in high school, except that I wish I had access to a one-stop shop for simplified breakdowns like these.
This being said, I noticed myself getting caught up in the technicalities behind some concepts, such as the difference between a voicing and an inversion.
For example, the site defined voicings as chord shapes with multiple instances of the same note, but then the picture above shows inversions of Cmin7 without the repeating root note.
Maybe it's because I did jazz band for three years and the band director glossed over this, but I'd like to really figure this out at some point.

On a less semantic-related note, I liked the breakdown of the "Around the World"
I remember trying to learn it on bass once and having some difficulty with it, because I had to jump around the fretboard to play it
But seeing it on the sequencer made it feel a lot more tame, and to be fair it does just ascend three times and descends at the last bar
It's still one of my favorite basslines and I never would have derived those chords from it alone-without listening to the entire track
Speaking of repeating phrases, the song "Award Tour" had me thinking a bit about how many phrases are enough to make a good song.
I've always liked A Tribe Called Quest since the first time I was introduced to them, and although the phrases they tend to sample might
seem like a few, their sample choices always seem to catch my ear even as I listen through their lyrics.
I'm one of those guitarists that tends to 'noodle' a lot and have a habit of making up a lot of new phrases when I make songs.
Listening to the Tribe track, however, brought me back to the value of simplicity.
