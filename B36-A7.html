Sponsorship Blogpost

I mostly chose these two local businesses because I have some familiarity or relation to them.
While seeking sponsorship from KVPrint might make a bit more sense (based on their business),
I do have a somewhat strong hunch that it could work with a business such as Game Haven.

KVPrint is an apparel printing company that is a sister company to Bakersfield Signs.
My dad currently works at Bakersfield Signs and while that’s a big reason for why I looked into this company,
I don’t actually know if they have considered sponsoring independent artists in the past before.
I do know that they have sponsored an artist for the Via Arte competition in town so maybe the managers could come around to sponsoring musicians.
As fas as KVPrint’s business goes, they mostly apparel products and design services to companies.
However, if I can introduce to them a potential customer base of young, DIY-type of fans interested in screen-printed custom shirts or sweaters,
I might have something to bounce off of for a deal. If KVPrint offers something in return such as free or discounted apparel, then I wouldn’t have to worry much about buying clothes.

As for Game Haven, I chose this business because after talking to one of the people who run the store, I learned that they have only been up and running since November.
They are fairly new and every time I go there with friends, there aren’t many people there despite the fact that they are located downtown.
I feel like they could strongly benefit from publicity and while this might not be immediately obvious,
I get a feeling that at least some people who listen to my Latin goth metal music might really be into fantasy board games or card games
(I found out last year after going to shops that a lot of older Hispanic males are really into Pokemon cards, for example).
While Game Haven might have a lot to gain if I bring them a whole new wave of clientele, I’m not much into collecting or playing anything so I couldn’t gain much from that end.
However, they do have some rentable rooms and a selection of snacks and drinks, so a deal based around those might actually be pretty good
(especially a deal on room time since I could host important or casual meetings there without sending people over to my place).
