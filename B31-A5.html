Archaeologist

"Demise":

Under the night
Across the sand
Lies a heart that no other could bind
To be found again and wander alone
Is out of mind, if not to suffer more

Absent of stars
Through winds that burn
I see your image over waters that fade
Ever-present thirst is nothing to fight
Once I find, what was mine before

I had this song idea for a long time now but always struggled to give it the right lyrics. Listening to it always gives me a sensation of someone wandering a desert in the midst of delirium. It's not just the occasional mirage that this person hallucinates, but, in a more metaphorical sense, visions and ideas of a past lover that this person can't come to grips with. This person is seeking something in a vast desert that they could never obtain before falling victim to the desert's unbareable conditions.
While I did have some sort of pre-existing idea about the lyircs, this whole concept fell into the territory of 'I am lost and dying without you.' I would have to say this is the underlying universal feeling, except that I guess I kind of worked backwards in a way. However, I didn't want to limit myself to phrases like 'I'm lost in a desert without you' or 'I'm wandering around and dying of thirst without you,' that sort of thing. As ususal, I wanted to strive for a good degree of vagueness with the lyrics, but I will have to admit this also was challenging with the mental image I strived to paint for this song.
I tried focusing on details a bit more. Lines such as 'through winds that burn' and 'ever-present thirst' suggest some sort of literal suffering that is really meant to be metaphorical. I think they complement the music well enough while possibly adding some level of vagueness as to whether the suffering in question is literal or not.
