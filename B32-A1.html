I haven't been tweaking synths at a profund level so reviewing the Ableton guide definitely helped with a few things I've been confused about.
The first thing being decay and sustain. It might sound a bit obvious but both just felt like the same to me for a while.
Of course, the big distinction to make is that decay refers to how the sound changes after the attack, up until reaching the sustain level.
This gets a bit tricky, but basically the sustain level is what your sound ends up like while you're still pressing on to a note.
For example, immediately after pressing a key, the pitch might go up to A2 and then down to D1. Once it stays at D1, that would
mean we've reached the sustain level, and I get this D1 pitch up until I let go. The I reach the release level of sound evolution.

I had a good refresher on the theme of envelope generators.
Of course envelopes can be used to change pitch or amplitude, but I'm wondering if some synths might have envelopes for changing other parts of a sound.
I guess one parameter could be the LFO- maybe there are some envelopes that let you tweak the LFO rate over time or something like that.
Generally, LFO parameters (other than rate and level) have been hard for me to wrap my head around. I'm hoping I can see some more examples of
advance LFO usage soon.. However, I'm not too sure if I'll be tweaking envelopes or LFO a lot when it comes to my own original music.
