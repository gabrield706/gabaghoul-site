Assignment 1

Question 1:
I want to blow people's minds.
This, at it's core, is why I want to follow (or at least try my hand at) a career in music,
I love creating sonic soundscapes that get people asking themselves 'how the heck did come up with this and why does this actually sound good?'
Whether it's an orchestral ballad with distorted, electronic percussion or a flanger-heavy guitar drone backed by afro-Latin percussion, I just love the idea of creating sounds that sound nothing like whatever tends to top the charts.
This isn't to say that I dislike pop music -I've got my fair share of guilty pleasure pop songs as many of us do-but really,
this comes from a love of playing with sounds as much as it comes from a place of four-chord pop song fatigue.
You might be wondering by now where this interest in sound experimentation came from, and really,
it came from my household just as is the case for the many people's musical influences.
Both of my parents listened to a lot of rock en español (rock in Spanish)-bands like Soda Stereo, Enanitos Verdes, Virus, La Ley, and Maná- but they also listened to good chunk of electronic stuff in English:
Artists such as OMD, Pet Shop Boys, Björk, Depeche Mode, and some other stuff in between.
Seeing a lot of those names, I guess I did grow up listening to a wide array of things,
and I just really want to mush a lot of those weird, crazy sounds together and share them with the world.

Question 2:
To me, making it means putting out at least four original albums throughout my lifetime, each with a sound that I can proudly be satisfied with.
I'm going with four albums because I do want to approach music with a mindset of quality over quantity-I'd rather be known as an artist with few but really exceptional pieces of work.
Artists like Tame Impala or Boards of Canada come to mind, they currently have somewhere around this number of albums, whereas big-name artists like Kanye West or Taylor Swift will tend to put an album out every two years.
Now, a good number of albums from the bigger-name artists might actually be really good, but from what I've seen,
artists with longer discographies tend to have fanbases that are more divided over the best era of said artist.
I guess I just really value the idea of concsistency across my body of work, and I get that this might not be the most lucrative approach to music.
However, pushing sonic barriers and cultivating a sizeable audience from that, really is 'making it for me'.
I'm not necessarily going into this with the idea of making money-this is why I am exploring audio career options,
and even why I spent six years getting an engineering degree.

Question 3:
As far as a music marathon, this is definitely a tricky one to answer, but this is somewhat relevant to the previous paragraph:
In one year, I think I could have learned enough to self-produce a demo EP to put up on the internet.
Since I am currently working part-time, I won't pretend like I'll have all the time in the world to put out a first album, so I'm totally fine settling for an EP.
I already have song demos I recorded throughout the years and I know that I can polish up at least four solid tracks before the end of the year.
I five years, assuming I've been able to network well and foster some opportunities within the industry, I might just have one or two albums out by now.
The first album, however, might just include some final recordings of the four EP tracks.
In 10 years, regardless of how I've faired financially with my own music, I should have album number three out by now.
As I would have had experience from the first two albums at this stage, I would most likely have learned enough about audio production to really make this album sound studio-crisp.
Because I am aiming for four albums, hopefully I would have cultivated at least a sizeable cult-following by now.
After 16 years since album number three, some of these devoted fans might be longing for the fourth and final album to come out.
I realize that a lot of time would have passed since the first album, and although I am concerned about consistency across material,
this seems like a good time to drop album number four and put out a more fresh and final take on my musical journey.
