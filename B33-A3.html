Assignment 3 - EQ Ear Training

I based my stage plot and input list off of the band I'm in.
We formed only a few months ago and haven't played any events yet, so I thought this assignment
would be a great opportunity to model what that would look like.
We're a four-piece band that currently plays post-punk, indie, and yacht rock,
and some of us have more than one role in the band (I kept the other band members' names anonymous)
While I only play guitar in the band (sometimes lead and sometimes rhythm), our frontman
also switches between guitar and keys while singing. Our bassist does backing vocals as well
and our drumer has a pretty standard setup. I based the plot off of an example from the reference site
as well as from our own practice setup. While we don't have that many monitors, I figured we would certainly need
that many. We currently don't have DI boxes for the bass and keyboard, but of course we'll need that for line leveling.

The input list was based off of that same setup; the most basic mic setup for the drummer
would be the two overhead mics and the kick drum, while everything else was pretty straightforward.
I treated every mix as if they represented the mixes going into each band member's monitor:
1 for the drummer, 2 for the bassist, 3 for myself, and 4 for the frontman.
I figured that for the drummer's monitor mix, they would need to not only hear themselves but the bassist
as well, since the bassist is also in charge of contributing to the rhythm. Similarly, it would be more essential
for the bassist to listen to the drummer for rhythmic guidance, as well as the vocals to get a sense of lyrical progress.
As the primary guitarist (myself), I felt like it would make more sense for me to hear the kick drum and the two vocals
to get the most barebones guidance for performing songs; however, as I look back I'm thinking that getting the bass in the mix
would really help towards identifying key changes. Last but not least, I included the kick and my guitar into the frontman's mix
to help avoid any overlapping guitar parts. He should also be able to listen to both vocals for the same reason, but I would also
retroactively include the bass track to help with key signatures.
