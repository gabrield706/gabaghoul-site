Podcast Assignment

My approach to soundtracking the podcast I did with Garrett was to simply add expressive sound effects
While we completely forgot about using the sound effects on the interface, I added some of in addition to the original synth sounds
Overall, everything added to the podcast was meant to highlight some section of the conversation or to further set the tone of it.

There's not that many sections where you'll hear a consistent piece of music in the background, but there is one notable exception:
There's a part in the podcast where I talk about attending BC years ago, where you'll hear a whole-step scale in the background.
I really wanted to land that feeling of a flashback or reminiscing a bygone time, so that's why that synth sound goes for as long as it does.
This is something I did using Ableton's drift synth, where I only needed to use 2 since oscillators with a long release to get something bell-like.

Something else that I did was to add little nods to some of the bands or genres we've brought up.
There's a section where Garrett talks about worship music, over which I added a C major chord pad to emphasize that.
My intent with that wasn't necessarily to be funny but to be tongue-in-cheek, which is also how I went about emphasizing other aspects of the podcast.
There's also a section where we talk about thrash metal and this was a bit hard for me to soundtrack-because I wanted to get a little thrash riff playing over that
and designing sounds with synths that are thrash-adjacent was not super intuitive for me. I got some inspiration from the 90's DOOM soundtrack since they literally
turned famous thrash and grunge songs into 8-bit-esque versions. That's a big reason for why the thrash bits sound as vintage as they do.

Honestly, working on this assignment really put my sense of what things sound like into practice.
I guess it was a bit easy in the sense that I could figure out what I wanted things to sound like-
sometimes I think of little sound bytes whenever I'm talking to someone and that definitel helped with coming up with melodies-
but actually getting the synths to sound exactly how I wanted them to was something else. Like I definitely knew what to do for a lot of
what I set out to make, but just as with many things in life, I think this is something i'll get better at with time.
Overall, I had a lot of fun!
