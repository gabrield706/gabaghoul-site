Synth Assignment 2

The tutorial video on the Korg Volca Sample can be found here: https://youtu.be/B7HqqrwbW3M

I made a small recording using only the Korg Volca Sample and some DAW effects.
Rather that playing different instruments on each track, I played a different sequence on each to create more interesting mixes.
The overall result feels more experimental that straightforward but I enjoyed making it nonetheless.

I had a lot of fun with the Volca's setp jump mode, the stutters were so fun and easy to do which is a bit odd,
because hearing stutters like that always gave me the impression of something that was hard to program for some reason.
Because of that feature though, I really leaned into an Aphex Twin/Björk kind of sound for that recording.

I didn't think much of it, but adding overdrive to that stuttery percussion really colored the sound more.
The stutters were done over the clap sequence from the 'claps' track, and those stutters just sound much more agressive.

I used a new sequence for the melodic track, which I made using a square wave sample and the motion sequence function.
That also had its own kick drum but adding some flanger gave it a bit more of a 'squishy' sound to it.
I normally don't go for more experimental electronic sounds like those, but I just went with the Björk vibe on that one.
Overall, even after buying a unit, I'm still amazed by what's possible with the Volca Sample.
As someone who eventually wants to play his original stuff live, I feel like the Volca would be a great option for integrating
fast and heavy electronic percussion sounds.
